Voter Identification Laws in the United States

Authored by Hunt Howard

This map is in the Web Mercator (ESPG: 3857) coordinate reference system and was made using CARTO by gathering, filtering, and rendering data obtained from The National Conference of State Legislators.

About Voter Identification and this Map

In the United States there are a total of 35 states that have some sort of identification requirement to be eligble to vote. In the map above you can, at first glance, compare the colors of each state to the legend to see the identification requirements that are or are not in place. You can also click on the respective state of interest to get the requirements or lack there of. To see Alaska and Hawaii please either zoom out in the window above or enlarge the map for easier viewing.

Implications of Voter Identification Laws

If identification requirements are too strict or too lenient it can effect voter turnout rates- either way altering polling numbers. We know that if the requirements cannot be met there are alternatives in place but often these methods are not known by the voter or are seen as too much trouble to be worth the voters time. For more information about how a voter can cast a ballot in an alternative way without identification (if required) please visit the NCSL website.
